Mid-Game Guide for CraftersMC Players

As a mid-game player, your primary focus should be unlocking minion slots, not just earning coins. The goal is to unlock 15 minion slots, which requires obtaining 200 unique minions.

1. Craft Minions and Use a Jungle Axe

  • Grind for Oak, Dark Oak, Spruce, Birch, Acacia, and Jungle Minions and upgrade each to Tier 9.
  • Use a Jungle Axe (available for around 5k coins) to speed up wood collection.
  • This will contribute 45 unique minion unlocks.

2. Maximize Your Pumpkin Farm

  • If you already have a Pumpkin Farm, create one maxed-out Pumpkin Minion and sell it.
  • Use the proceeds to fund upgrades for 11 additional minion slots.

3. Build Beetroot and Potato Farms

  • On your island, create Beetroot and Potato Farms.
  • Grind for Enchanted Beetroot and Enchanted Potatoes to craft and upgrade their respective minions to Tier 9.
  • This will unlock 18 unique slots.

4. Visit the Fungi Cave

  • Head to the Fungi Cave to unlock the recipes for Redstone Minions and Lapis Minions.
  • Place these minions on your island and upgrade them to contribute an additional 10 slots.

5. Grind in the Craven Lobby

  • Visit the Craven Lobby to mine Iron, Gold, and Coal.
  • Unlock the recipes for their respective minions, craft them, and place them on your island.
  • Upgrade Redstone, Lapis, Coal, Iron, and Gold Minions to Tier 9.

6. Maximize Cobblestone Minion

  • Grind cobblestone on your island to max out a Cobblestone Minion.

7. Unlock the Duck Minion

  • Grind ducks in Harvest Heaven to unlock the Duck Minion recipe.

8. Ideal Minion Setup

Once you have unlocked 15 minion slots, place the following on your island:

  • 2 Redstone Minions
  • 1 Lapis Minion
  • 2 Coal Minions
  • 2 Iron Minions
  • 2 Gold Minions
  • 2 Duck Minions
  • 2 Dark Oak Minions
  • 2 Oak Minions

With this setup, you will have fully unlocked 15 minion slots, setting you up for further progression in CraftersMC.

  • Reach Farming Level 21.
  • Reach Foraging Level 20.
  • Reach Mining Level 20.

2. Slayer XP Grinding

Follow this Slayer guide step-by-step:

  • Start Grinding Slayer:
    • I recommend you to Check out this Slayer Guide Video.
    • Do not keep extra coins in your wallet to avoid losses if you die.
  • Grind till Slayer Level 4: Then get yourself a Revenant Armor.
  • Then grind till level 5.

3. Coin Grinding for Upgrades

Once you hit Slayer Level 5:

  • Farm Setup:
    • Build a 161x161 Beetroot Farm (this will take time).
  • Daily Farming:
    • Grind Enchanted Beetroot and Enchanted Cocoa.
    • Aim for 20 stacks of each daily.
    • Build a Cocoa Farm with 40x40 rows because it grows quickly and also helpful for increasing your skill level.
    • Sell them in the Bazaar.
  • Gear Upgrades:
    • Purchase a Frozen Blaze Helmet.
    • Grind Wolf Slayer XP until Level 3.
    • Craft a Maddox Batphone for easy Slayer access.
    • Buy a Piggy Bank to safely store coins.

4. Focus on Minion Slots and Coin Farming

  • Unlock Minion Slots:
    • Unlock up to the 19th minion slot after reaching 15 slots.
    • Use coins to upgrade minions to speed up progress and unlock more slots.
  • Avoid Expensive Minions:
    • Do not max out expensive minions like Cow, Pig, Slime, Sugar, Cactus, or Rabbit.
    • Tiers 7–9 are sufficient for these.
  • Maxed Magma Minion Setup:
    • Obtain 17 Maxed Magma Minions with:
      • Medium Backpack
      • Super Compactor 3000
      • Enchanted Lava Bucket
      • Diamond Spreading
    • Add 1 Dark Oak Minion and 1 Redstone Minion.
    • Earnings: With this setup, you will make around 800k coins per day by selling Magma Cream directly to NPCs.

5. Last thing for Slayer

  • Use your Frozen Blaze Helmet instead of a Zombie Heart for efficient Tier 4 Zombie Slayer grinding.
  • Focus on ensuring you can complete Zombie T4 Bosses consistently and easily.
  • Grind till Slayer lvl 6 and get yourself a Reaper Sword .

In Short: Learn to efficiently grind Tier 4 Slayer bosses and set up a passive income with optimized farms and minions.

    Bazaar Guide for CraftersMC Players

    The Bazaar will be an active trading center for CraftersMC players, enabling efficient buying and selling of commodity items. This guide will break down the mechanics and strategies to help you maximize your profits and efficiency while using the Bazaar.

    Understanding the Bazaar

    The Bazaar is categorized into five main sections:

    • Farming
    • Mining
    • Combat
    • Woods
    • Miscellaneous

    Each category contains specific items with buy and sell prices based on supply and demand. You can toggle between two modes:

    • Direct Mode: Displays only buy/sell prices.
    • Advanced Mode: Shows additional data like buy/sell order volumes and recent transaction history (7-day insights).

    Trading Options in the Bazaar

    Once you click on a product, you have four primary options:

    1. Buy Instantly

    • Use this to purchase items immediately at the lowest available price.
    • The Bazaar charges 4% above the lowest price for stability. Any excess is refunded if the transaction completes at a lower cost.
    • Ensure your inventory has enough space before buying.

    2. Sell Instantly

    • Quickly sell all items in your inventory to the best buy orders.
    • Right-clicking allows you to choose a specific quantity to sell.

    3. Create Buy Order

    • Set a quantity and unit price for items you want to buy.
    • Presets: 64, 160, 1,024 for stackable items; 1, 4, 16 for unstackable ones.
    • Pricing Options: Match current highest, increase by 0.1 coins, or set a custom price.
    • Orders not immediately fulfilled stay active until filled or canceled.

    4. Create Sell Offer

    • Similar to buy orders, but for selling.
    • Pricing Options: Match the best, reduce by 0.1 coins, or set a custom price.
    • All items in your inventory for that product type will be placed up for sale.

    Managing Orders

    Use the Manage Orders menu to track and adjust your buy/sell orders:

    • View stats like items sold, coins collected, and % completion.
    • Left-click to collect items/coins, or right-click to cancel incomplete orders.
    • Fully completed buy orders can be flipped into sell offers.

    Note:

    • Orders expire after 7 days.
    • A maximum of 14 orders can be active simultaneously (expandable with the Bazaar Flipper perk).
    • The sell cap is 1 billion coins worth of items at a time.

    Understanding Fees and Taxes

    • A 1.25% tax applies to all Bazaar sales.
    • The Bazaar Flipper perk reduces taxes by 0.125% per level and increases order capacity.

    Advanced Tools

    Graphs

    • Access historical data like buy/sell prices, order volumes, and transaction trends.
    • Time ranges: 24 hours, 7 days, or 31 days.
    • Avoid relying on graphs for predictions, as they have a 1-day delay.

    Bazaar Trading Strategies

    1. Profit from Price Fluctuations

    • Bazaar Flipping: Buy low and sell high. Use graphs to identify price dips or spikes.
    • Example: Buy Enchanted Rotten Flesh at a low price and create a sell offer at a higher rate.

    2. Bulk Orders for Discounts

    • Create buy orders for large quantities instead of buying instantly. This allows you to get items at a lower cost.

    3. Focus on High-Demand Items

    • Items like Super Compactor 3000 often have steady demand and good margins.

    4. Use Presets for Efficiency

    • Utilize preset quantities and pricing to save time while creating orders or offers.

    5. Avoid Overpricing

    • Keep your offers competitive by matching or slightly undercutting existing prices.

    Epic Rarity and Above:

    • Strong. Not even a contest. At Epic Rarity, it matches the total STR + CD number of a Forceful or Hurtful, and at Legendary and Special, the STR + CD number is actually higher. Plus, you get extra defense, so why the heck not?
    • If you’re desperate for AS, Strange on Epics.

    Rare Rarity:

    If Strength is Too High:

    • Hurtful or Itchy. Itchy gives a slightly lower total buff, but the increase in AS might be appealing.

    If Crit Damage is Too High:

    • Forceful.

    If Strength and Crit Damage are close to Equal:

    • Alternate between Forceful and Hurtful. It doesn’t matter which one is prioritized.

    Common or Uncommon Rarity:

    If Strength is Too High:

    • Hurtful or Itchy.

    If Crit Damage is Too High:

    • Forceful.

    If Strength and Crit Damage are close to Equal:

    • Superior.

    Uncommons Only:

    If you want to focus on Attack Speed, Strange isn’t a bad pick.

    Commons Only:

    If you want to focus on Crit Chance, go for Unpleasant.

    1. At what collection will I get Legendary Rock Pet?

    You need to grind 450k+ blocks to obtain the Legendary Rock Pet. While grinding, you'll also acquire Common, Uncommon, Rare, and Epic Rock Pets. Sell these additional pets for extra coins.

    2. What to do with Enchanted Cobblestone?

    Avoid selling the Enchanted Cobblestone you collect during the grind. Instead, use it to craft Super Compactors and place them in your minions to boost their productivity.

    3. What to do with coins?

    Be cautious with your spending and avoid wasting coins. Save the coins earned from selling Rock Pets to support your future upgrades and progression.
